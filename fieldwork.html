Anyuan Hakka Documentation Project

An ongoing community-based project centered on Anyuan Hakka, a lesser-studied Hakka variety spoken in Anyuan, Jiangxi, China. Since 2021, the project has brought together language documentation, community collaboration, and public-facing language education.

Project Vision

Language, memory, and everyday life

Born and raised in Anyuan, I speak Anyuan Hakka, a lesser-studied variety of the Hakka language. This project documents, analyzes, preserves, and promotes Anyuan Hakka through collaboration with local community members and through attention to language as it is lived in everyday settings.

The project treats language not as an isolated system, but as something deeply connected to place, migration, local history, foodways, built environments, and intergenerational memory. It also aims to make linguistic knowledge accessible to community members who may not have access to English-language academic resources.

Community Work

Speaking Anyuan Hakka together

Chatting Group

I run an online Anyuan Hakka chatting group with younger generations from Anyuan. The group meets weekly and creates a space for people living away from home to continue speaking Anyuan Hakka and maintain their proficiency.

Language Course

In 2024, I developed and taught an introductory general linguistics course in Anyuan Hakka for community members without prior linguistics training.

Public Access

The project shares linguistic ideas in accessible forms so that documentation can also serve community learning, reflection, and continued language use.
